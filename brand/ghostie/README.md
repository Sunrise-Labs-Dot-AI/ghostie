# Ghostie Brand Guidelines

Ghostie is a friendly little ghost with two jobs: help you stop ghosting people, and ghost-write replies in your own voice for your approval. The brand should feel warm, candid, playful, and quietly protective. It should never feel clinical, spooky, or corporate.

## Logo

Use the full lockup when the brand needs introduction, use the mark in tight UI, menu bar, favicon, and app icon contexts. Clear space equals the ghost eye diameter on all sides of the mark, or the height of the capital G bowl around the wordmark. Do not crop the ghost, tilt the wordmark, add new facial details, or place the mark on busy photography.

## Mascot

The mascot is built on a 96 by 96 vector grid. Keep the same body silhouette, two round eyes, cheek dots, rounded stroke caps, and three bottom scallops. Expressions may change eyes, mouth, arms, and small props only. The ghost can be happy, winking, sleepy, waving, saying boo, or thinking. Do not make the ghost scary, sharp, transparent to the point of looking inactive, or more detailed than the UI around it.

## Color

The core palette is warm cream, ink plum, spirit teal, friend coral, honey, and lilac. Cream and teal carry the consumer door. Ink plum and mint carry the power door. Use coral sparingly for cheeks, emotional tone, nudges, and share moments. Text pairs and component pairs in `tokens.css` are selected for accessible contrast in both light and dark mode.

## Type

Display: Fraunces, 800 to 900 weight, used for brand moments, Wrapped cards, and landing hero headlines.

Body: Inter, 400 to 700 weight, used for UI, marketing copy, settings, forms, and cards.

Mono: JetBrains Mono, 500 to 700 weight, used only for power-door labels, API key hints, model names, timestamps, and logs.

Hierarchy: hero 96 to 116, display 56 to 76, title 32 to 44, heading 22 to 26, body 16, small 14, caption 12. Keep letter spacing at 0.

## Components

Primary buttons use ink plum in light mode and mint in dark mode. Secondary buttons use quiet surfaces with teal text. Draft awaiting approval cards use honey backgrounds, a visible approval badge, and explicit language that the user approves before sending. Message bubbles should look familiar and soft, but never copy iMessage or WhatsApp exactly.

## Two Doors

Consumer door: warm, playful, free to start. The mascot is expressive and close to the copy. Lead with friendship, birthdays, follow-ups, emotional tone, and Texting Wrapped.

Power door: minimal, premium, advanced. The same mascot appears as an outline, badge, or small operator signal. Lead with BYOK, local reading, drafting, automation, and approval-gated sends.

## Voice

Write like a candid friend who is helpful before they are clever. Be warm, direct, and a little cheeky. Say "You approve before anything sends." Avoid fear, surveillance language, enterprise jargon, and moral scolding. Do not use em dashes in brand copy.
