# Design Context — Ghostie (menu bar app)

Brand-grounded direction for every UI surface. Supersedes the "calm native precision"
direction (pre-rebrand): Ghostie committed to a warmer, character-led identity in the
0.7.0 rebrand. This is the single point of view every surface must converge on.
Sources of truth: `brand/ghostie/README.md` (voice + mascot rules), `brand/ghostie/tokens.css`
(palette), `brand/ghostie/sprites/` (the canonical pixel mascot), the landing page
(`site/ghostie/index.html`), and the product stance in `CLAUDE.md`.

## Users
Everyday macOS users (not developers) who text a lot and want help being a better friend:
answering the texts they keep meaning to answer, remembering birthdays, replying in their
own voice. Often privacy-conscious. They live in the menu bar between other tasks; sessions
are short and interruptible. A smaller power-user segment connects Claude/MCP agents (the
"power door") — same gate, quieter dial.

## Brand personality
**Warm. Candid. A little cheeky. Quietly protective.** A candid friend who is helpful before
they are clever. Never clinical, spooky, corporate, or scolding. The approval gate is the
center of the brand: "AI writes it. You send it. In that order." No fear or surveillance
language. No em dashes in brand copy.

## The two doors (one product, different dials)
- **Consumer door (default):** warm, playful, mascot is expressive and close to the copy.
  Leads with friendships, birthdays, quiet threads, Wrapped.
- **Power door (Settings → AI Tools, MCP surfaces):** same palette, quieter application;
  the mascot appears small or as an outline; mono type is allowed here for real data.
Never average the two into one mushy middle. A surface should know which door it lives in.

## Aesthetic direction — "warm paper, crisp ink"
- **Typeface:** SF Pro stays for ALL native UI chrome (native correctness beats brand
  novelty in a menu bar app). Fraunces/Silkscreen are WEB/MARKETING faces — do not embed
  custom display fonts in the app. Character comes from color, the mascot, and copy.
- **Color (canonical = `DS.Color` ghostie tokens, mirrored from `brand/ghostie/tokens.css`):**
  warm cream surfaces (`#FFF8F1` light shell), ink `#211A2A`; **spirit teal `#007D73`** is the
  primary accent (replaces iMessage blue `#0A7CFF` — blue now appears ONLY inside message
  bubbles and platform glyphs); **honey `#FFD66D`/`#FFF0C2`** is reserved for the approval
  gate (badges, staged-draft cards, hold-to-fire) — honey = "waiting for you" everywhere,
  never decoration; coral `#E85D4F` for warmth accents and destructive-adjacent warnings;
  WhatsApp green only on WhatsApp identity. Dark mode is plum-tinted near-black, not gray.
  One palette, defined once in `DesignSystem/DSColor.swift` — no view-private color enums.
- **The mascot:** pixel-grid Ghostie sprites (`brand/ghostie/sprites/`) at small, deliberate
  moments: sidebar contextual mark, empty states, onboarding, celebration. Render crisp
  (integer scaling, `interpolation(.none)`), never stretched, never more detailed than the
  UI around it. Motion may float or blink; it must NEVER imply the app is sending by itself.
- **Depth:** soft, generous elevation on sheets/popovers/emphasis cards. The neo-brutalist
  offset-shadow style (hard 3-4pt ink shadow) is a *selected-state and marketing* accent —
  used consistently where used (sidebar selection, landing page), not sprinkled.
- **Space & rhythm:** `DS.Space` 4pt scale, varied (tight within a group, generous between
  groups). Uniform `.padding(12)` everywhere is a slop tell.
- **Motion:** purposeful, ease-out, brief; honor Reduce Motion. One orchestrated moment
  beats scattered micro-interactions.

## Anti-patterns to refuse
1. **Don't wrap everything in cards / stack identical cards.** Group with spacing + dividers;
   a card is for the one thing that needs emphasis (usually the approval gate).
2. **No side-stripe accent borders.** Full hairline borders, background tints, leading icons.
3. **Mono type only for real data** (timestamps, counts, keys, logs) and power-door labels —
   never consumer-surface chrome.
4. No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no center-everything, no every-button-primary.
5. **Bury technical nouns** (daemon, MCP, Baileys, PostHog, chat.db, paths → plain language
   or an Advanced disclosure). Full Disk Access stays; it's Apple's own pane name.
6. **Honey discipline:** if it's honey-colored, it must be waiting for the user's approval.
   If it isn't, it must not be honey.
7. The ghost is never scary, never transparent-to-invisible, never off-model (one canonical
   silhouette: dome head, 2x2 eyes, three-bump skirt, left blue highlight).

## The test
If someone said "an AI generated this UI," would you believe them? Goal: no. It should read
as a considered product with a real character — warm paper, crisp ink, one friendly ghost.

## Process note
Token substitution alone keeps flat, over-carded, uniform layouts — it stays sloppy. Real
fixes change structure (de-card, rhythm, depth, hierarchy) and are verified by LOOKING at
the rendered app, not just a green build.
